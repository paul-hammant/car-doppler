When audio is too loud, it gets "clipped" - the peaks get cut off, destroying the frequency information needed for Doppler analysis.

üéØ Quick Fixes

  • Move further away: Increase distance from the road
  • Avoid very loud vehicles: Skip air horns, loud exhausts, sirens
  • Check phone settings: Some phones have mic gain settings
  • Remove phone case: Cases can amplify and distort sound
  • Point away slightly: Don't aim directly at extremely loud sources

üîä Optimal Audio Levels

The best recordings have:

  • Clear audio: Loud enough to hear the vehicle clearly
  • No distortion: No crackling or harsh sounds
  • Consistent volume: Not maxing out the microphone
  • Good signal-to-noise ratio: Vehicle sound over background noise

üöõ Vehicle Selection

Good targets (moderate volume):

  • Regular passenger cars
  • Standard delivery trucks
  • City buses (from appropriate distance)

Avoid (too loud):

  • Vehicles with modified exhausts
  • Emergency vehicles with sirens
  • Large trucks with air brakes
  • Motorcycles with loud pipes

üìç Distance Guidelines

Adjust your distance based on vehicle type:

  • Cars: 5-10 feet from road edge
  • Trucks: 10-15 feet from road edge
  • Loud vehicles: 15-20 feet from road edge

üîß Technical Details

Audio clipping occurs when the input signal exceeds the microphone's maximum input level. This:

  • Flattens the audio waveform peaks
  • Introduces harmonic distortion
  • Destroys the precise frequency information needed for Doppler analysis
  • Makes speed calculation impossible
